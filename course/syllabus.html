    Course Description.

    An opportunity for supervised practicum field experience in mental health counseling. Guided by a cooperating Agency and University faculty, students participate in aspects of the counseling process which may include assessment, diagnosis and treatment, ethical considerations, and use of self in the counseling process. Connections between this practicum, the counseling internship, and the counseling field are discussed.
    🔗

    Prerequisites.

    CPSY 901 Theories of Psychotherapy and Counseling, CPSY 911 Orientation to Counseling Practice, CPSY 921 Professional Issues in Counseling and Mental Health, CPSY 925 Psychopathology, and permission of the Program Coordinator.

    Definitions of terms in Syllabus and Graduate Catalog.

    Counseling Practicum Student: Is a graduate student in mental health counseling who is completing their professional preparation for the Master of Arts with a concentration in Mental Health Counseling.
    🔗
    Practicum Site Supervisor: The counseling supervisor at the practicum site who meets Massachusetts state license guidelines. The primary responsibility of the site supervisor is to direct/supervise the day-to-day experiences of the practicum student.
    🔗
    University Faculty Supervisor/Practicum Instructor: The University supervisor will be a member of the counseling faculty of Framingham State University. The responsibility of the university supervisor is to meet regularly with both the practicum student and the site supervisor, support the professional activities of the practicum student during the practicum experience; meet with the practicum students in regularly scheduled seminars; and meet with the site supervisor as needed and for a final evaluation of the practicum student.

    Class Activities.

    Recorded Role-plays where you will be provided instructor and peer feedback
    🔗
    Theory to practice conversations using assigned readings and videos
    🔗
    Case presentations related to your practicum experience (questions/feedback, clinical guidance, ethical issues, reading discussion)

    Reading Discussion and Breakout Activity Groups

    Course and Practicum Objectives.

    Demonstrate sensitivity to differences and cultural diversity in the work setting with clients.
    🔗
    Establish and maintain a client observation/shadow schedule.
    🔗
    Develop goal setting and treatment planning skills.
    🔗
    Demonstrate an ability to hear and understand content and effect of client messages.
    🔗
    Examine agency mission, policies, programs, and surrounding community of the practicum.
    🔗
    Expand and apply theoretical knowledge appropriate to client population, setting and purpose of the internship setting.
    🔗
    Demonstrate a commitment to legal, professional, ethical standards, and function within them.
    🔗
    Consistently exhibit personal and behavioral traits conducive to effective counseling, learning, and professional growth.
    🔗
    Demonstrate professional behavior at the practicum site, establishing and maintaining effective working relationships with clients, supervisors, and colleagues.
    🔗
    Demonstrate an understanding of client records, testing and reports that reflects sensitivity and attention to equity and antiracism.
    🔗
    Maintain a practicum log for documentation of professional activities.
    🔗
    Reflect upon and evaluate interactions with clients, supervisors, and colleagues within the practicum setting.
    🔗
    Develop clinical hypotheses and case formulations.
    🔗
    Practicum evaluation, process recordings, course readings, seminar discussions, learning contract, Time2Track reports and activity logs, reflection paper and case presentations
    🔗

    Course Requirements/Student Responsibilities.

            40 hours of direct client contact (face to face or virtual contact only).
            🔗
            10 hours of individual supervision by an approved supervisor.
            🔗
            5 hours of group supervision by an approved supervisor or university supervisor (*1.5hrs of group seminar time is counted each month as part of group sup hours).
            🔗
            10 hours of either additional group or individual supervision or a mix of either.

                        Course Evaluation and GradingGrading Outline

                        Your grade for this course will be based on your attendance, active participation in seminars, documentation completion and your overall engagement and performance in your practicum setting. Consistent attendance, meaningful contributions to discussions, and demonstrating professionalism and growth in your practicum are all key factors in your final evaluation and will count toward your final grade. See Canvas module for full grading rubric.
                        🔗
                                  An “A” grade indicates that in addition to satisfactorily completing all the course requirements, the Practicum Student demonstrates exceptional counseling skills, consistently exhibits high standards of professional and personal behavior, demonstrates a consistent willingness to learn and to incorporate feedback in their work, is cooperative and resourceful in their work environment, and is highly committed to the counseling profession.
                                  🔗
                                  A “B” grade indicates that in addition to completing all the course requirements, the Practicum Student demonstrates strong counseling skills, consistently exhibits appropriate standards of professional and personal behavior, demonstrates a willingness to learn and to incorporate feedback in their work, is cooperative and resourceful in their work environment, and is committed to the counseling profession.

                                        Documentation GuidanceRecording of LecturesParticipation in Virtual Classroom

                                        Process recordings document observable behaviors, reactions, and perceived themes in student-client interactions. They promote analytical thinking, self-awareness, and the development of clinical relationships. Through this tool, site supervisors and students can collaboratively examine skill development, address potential countertransference, and assess the progression of clinical relationships. Unless otherwise specified by your site supervisor, follow the format outlined below for your process recordings. Process Recordings must maintain confidentiality; do not include real identifying information about clients or participants.
                                        🔗
                                        Refer to the sample process recording available in the Canvas files.The narrative must capture specific verbal and nonverbal communication. Start with a manageable section (e.g., 10-15 minutes of a session, group meeting, or individual counseling) until you gain confidence in recalling and documenting details.
                                        🔗
                                        This class will use video and audio recordings of faculty and students online to better support learning in a blended format. By enrolling in the course, students are consenting to being recorded in this class and may only withdraw such consent by informing the course instructor in writing. As these recordings may contain intellectual property as well as confidential student information (ex. student names, likenesses), sharing or transferring recordings of such content by any method currently available or any method that may become available in the future is not allowed, and copies of such recordings should not be provided to others; uploaded, linked, embedded, or otherwise posted via file-sharing, social media, or other sites or services which could enable anyone to view or hear them who is not currently enrolled in the course; or share them in any other way. Access to video and audio recordings in this class is for personal educational use only and is available only to individuals currently enrolled in the class, unless faculty permission is expressly granted. Recording and/or sharing course materials, including video and audio files, without the written consent of the course instructor is a violation of University policies (ex. academic honesty) and will be reported to the College Dean for further action and/or discipline.
                                        🔗
                                        Learning is an active process that requires engagement from both the professor and the learner. To achieve the course objectives, seminar interaction is essential. Given the course’s focus on the application of content, skill development, collaboration, and interpersonal learning, active verbal participation and attentive listening are expected.
                                        🔗
                                        Creating and sustaining a safe, inclusive, and engaging remote learning environment is a shared responsibility. To promote respect for both your learning process and that of your peers, please observe the following guidelines:
                                        🔗
                                        Engage Respectfully:  Challenge ideas, not the individuals presenting them. Separate ideas from people to foster constructive dialogue.  Personal attacks or dismissive responses hinder learning, limit perspective-taking, and undermine empathy.  Communicate Thoughtfully:  Online communication requires extra care to ensure messages are understood as intended.  Without non-verbal cues or voice tone, misunderstandings can occur. Ask clarifying questions and check perceptions to enhance understanding.  Discussing Sensitive Topics:  This course may address topics such as oppression, stigma, inequities, and bias, which may provoke strong emotional responses. Honesty and openness are vital as we work to unpack, analyze, and understand these issues.  Use “I” statements to express your thoughts, feelings, and beliefs (e.g., “I feel…” rather than “you” or “we”).  Respect privacy and confidentiality, and use person-first language (e.g., “person diagnosed with depression” rather than “depressed person”).  Managing Emotional Discomfort:  If course content elicits intense emotional discomfort, notify your instructor and/or seek support through counseling services at Framingham State University or another accessible organization.  Foster Inclusive Learning:  There are no “stupid” questions. If you have a question, others likely have the same concern.  Comments that demean, dismiss, or criticize others are not permitted in any form of communication.  If you observe or experience inappropriate behavior, notify the instructor.  Maintain Confidentiality:  Discussions within seminars should remain confidential to protect the integrity of the learning environment and the privacy of participants. By adhering to these principles, we can collectively create a respectful, inclusive, and effective learning space.
